# How to Filter Your Trades in TradeZella

TradeZella's powerful filtering system allows you to analyze specific subsets of your trading data, making it easy to evaluate individual strategies, symbols, time periods, or accounts separately. Whether you want to see only your breakout trades, analyze performance by month, compare different trading symbols, or review trades from a specific broker account, filters help you slice your data in meaningful ways. Combined with TradeZella's analytics, filtering enables deep performance analysis across unlimited dimensions.

The filter interface is intuitive and allows multiple simultaneous filters, so you can create precise queries like "all winning trades on ES from my scalping strategy in March 2024." Filtered views update all associated metrics, charts, and statistics in real-time, giving you immediate insight into the performance of your filtered trade subset. Filters are also shareable, allowing you to save and reuse your most common filter combinations.

## Basic Filtering Interface

### Accessing Filters
1. Navigate to your **Trade Journal** or **Analytics** page
2. Look for the **"Filter"** button near the top of the trades list (typically to the right of search)
3. Click to open the filter panel
4. Available filter options appear as dropdown menus and selection boxes

### Applying Filters
- Click on each filter category to expand it
- Select your filter criteria (can select multiple options)
- Results update automatically as you adjust filters
- The trades list and all associated metrics recalculate instantly

### Clearing Filters
- Click **"Clear All Filters"** to remove all active filters at once
- Click the **X** icon on individual filter chips to remove specific filters
- Refresh the page to reset to default (unfiltered) view

## Common Filter Types

### Date Range Filter
Filter trades by the date they were opened or closed:
- **Preset options:** This Week, This Month, Last 30 Days, This Year, Last Year
- **Custom range:** Select specific start and end dates
- **Useful for:** Monthly performance review, seasonal strategy analysis, comparing year-over-year performance

### Strategy Filter
Narrow down to specific trading strategies:
- Select one or more strategies you've tagged in TradeZella
- If you haven't tagged trades by strategy, consider adding this organizational method
- **Useful for:** Evaluating individual strategy performance, identifying which strategies are profitable, comparing edge between different approaches

### Symbol/Instrument Filter
Filter by specific trading symbols:
- Search for or select individual stocks, currency pairs, crypto assets, etc.
- Multi-select to compare performance across related symbols
- **Useful for:** Analyzing performance on specific symbols, identifying symbols where you struggle, comparing your success across different assets

### Account Filter
View trades from specific broker accounts or trading platforms:
- Select one or more accounts from your connected integrations
- Compare performance across different brokers
- **Useful for:** Troubleshooting account-specific issues, comparing broker fills, analyzing performance by broker

### Tags Filter
Filter by custom tags you've applied to trades:
- Select one or more tags to narrow results
- Create tags like "revenge trade," "news trade," "setup confirmed," "high-volatility," etc.
- **Useful for:** Analyzing outcomes of specific trade conditions, identifying problem patterns, evaluating disciplined vs. emotional trades

### Trade Type Filter
Filter by trade direction and status:
- **Long/Short:** View only long positions or short positions
- **Winning/Losing:** Show only profitable trades or losing trades
- **Open/Closed:** Display only open positions or completed trades

### Win/Loss Filter
- **Winning Trades:** Trades with positive profit
- **Losing Trades:** Trades with negative profit
- **Breakeven:** Trades closing near entry price
- **Useful for:** Analyzing what works vs. what doesn't, comparing winner quality to loser quality

## Advanced Filtering Scenarios

### Analyze Specific Strategy Performance
1. Select **Strategy:** "Momentum Breakout"
2. Select **Date Range:** "Last 90 Days"
3. View filtered analytics: win rate, avg win, avg loss, trade expectancy for only this strategy in this period
4. Compare these metrics to your overall performance to evaluate this strategy's edge

### Compare Symbols Performance
1. Multi-select **Symbols:** ES, NQ, YM (index futures)
2. View performance metrics to see which future you trade best
3. Remove symbols one at a time to isolate performance on specific instruments

### Identify Problem Patterns
1. Select **Trade Type:** "Losing Trades"
2. Add **Tags:** "revenge trade"
3. Analyze: Do your revenge trades lose more than average? By how much?
4. Use data to improve discipline

### Monthly Performance Review
1. Select **Date Range:** Specific month
2. View key metrics: Total trades, win rate, profit, R-multiple distribution
3. Compare to previous months to identify seasonal patterns or improvements
4. Break down by strategy to see which contributed most to the month's performance

## Saving and Managing Filters

### Save Filter Combinations
1. Set up your desired filter combination
2. Click **"Save Filter"** button
3. Give your filter a meaningful name: "ES Scalping Strategy," "Monthly Review," etc.
4. Click save

### Access Saved Filters
- Your saved filters appear in a dropdown menu near the filter button
- Quick-select to instantly apply frequently-used filters
- Filters are stored in your account and accessible across sessions

### Delete Saved Filters
- Click the **X** next to a saved filter name to remove it
- Deleted filters can be recreated anytime

## Best Practices for Filtering

**Tag Consistently:** Use consistent naming for strategies, tags, and accounts so filtering is reliable and meaningful.

**Regular Reviews:** Schedule monthly reviews with filters to analyze specific strategies and identify improvements.

**Compare Filtered to Overall:** When analyzing a filtered subset, compare those metrics to your overall metrics to see if that subset performs better or worse.

**Test Filters Before Trading:** Use filters to backtest or analyze historical performance of new strategies before risking capital.

**Document Filter Insights:** Keep notes on what your filters reveal—trading insights about your edge, weaknesses, and seasonal patterns.
