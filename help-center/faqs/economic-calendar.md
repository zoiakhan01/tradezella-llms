# Exploring the Economic Calendar in TradeZella

TradeZella includes an integrated economic calendar that displays major economic data releases, central bank announcements, and geopolitical events that impact financial markets. Rather than switching between multiple windows to check economic events, you can view the calendar directly within TradeZella and correlate major market moves with economic announcements. This integration helps traders understand the macro context of their trades and identify whether price action was driven by fundamental economic data or technical factors.

The economic calendar shows event dates, times, expected forecasts, previous readings, and actual results. Each event is tagged with an impact level (high, medium, or low) so you can quickly identify which announcements are likely to cause significant market volatility. Over time, you can review your trade journal to see how your trading performance correlates with economic calendar events—are you trading around major releases? Do you lose money on high-impact events? This analysis helps optimize your trading schedule.

## Accessing the Economic Calendar

### Opening the Calendar
1. Log into your TradeZella account
2. Look for **"Economic Calendar"** in the main navigation menu
3. Click to open the full calendar view
4. Default view shows the current week and upcoming events

### Calendar Views Available
- **Week View:** Shows upcoming 7 days of events
- **Month View:** Full month calendar of events
- **List View:** Chronological list of upcoming events with details

## Understanding Calendar Information

### Event Details Displayed

**Event Name:** The economic indicator or announcement (e.g., "Non-Farm Payroll," "Fed Interest Rate Decision," "Inflation CPI")

**Country/Region:** Which economy the event affects (US, Eurozone, UK, Japan, China, Canada, Australia, etc.)

**Date & Time:** When the event will be released, shown in your local timezone

**Previous Reading:** The value of this indicator from the last reporting period (for comparison)

**Forecast:** The median expectation from economists about what the actual number will be

**Actual Result:** The actual released figure (updated once the event occurs)

**Impact Level:**
- **High Impact:** Major economic data that typically causes significant market volatility. Examples: Non-Farm Payroll (US), ECB Interest Rate, Fed Funds Rate Decision
- **Medium Impact:** Important data with moderate market effect. Examples: Retail Sales, Manufacturing PMI, GDP
- **Low Impact:** Minor indicators with less market sensitivity

### Impact Interpretation
- **Better than Forecast = Bullish:** Actual result is stronger than expected (positive for that economy)
- **Worse than Forecast = Bearish:** Actual result is weaker than expected
- **As Forecast = Expected:** No surprise, minimal impact expected

## Using the Calendar in Trading

### Planning Your Trading Schedule
1. Review the **Week View** of the economic calendar
2. Identify **High Impact** events scheduled
3. Consider scaling back position sizes or avoiding trading 30 minutes before/after major announcements
4. Schedule recovery/review time after volatile economic events

### Identifying Event-Driven Trades
- Some traders specifically trade the volatility created by economic data releases
- Straddles or breakout strategies around major events
- The calendar helps you plan these event-driven setups in advance

### Avoiding News Spikes
- If you're a technical trader preferring orderly trends, review the calendar before your trading session
- Avoid trading during high-impact releases if volatility is detrimental to your strategy
- Place your trades to have time/space before major economic announcements

## Correlating Trades with Economic Events

### Analyzing Trade Performance vs. Events
1. Export your trades for a specific period
2. Note which trades occurred on or near major economic releases
3. Compare: Do your trades around high-impact events have different win rates or R-multiples?
4. Adjust trading plan based on findings

### Common Event-Trade Correlations
- **Large unexpected gaps:** Often correspond to major economic surprises or geopolitical events
- **Increased volatility:** Typically follows economic data releases or central bank decisions
- **Trend reversals:** Sometimes coincide with major macro announcements
- **Breakout trades:** Can be triggered by economic catalysts

### Building an Event Trading Strategy
1. Use calendar to identify consistent trading opportunities around specific releases
2. Test setup: "Does my technical setup + this economic catalyst = more consistent profits?"
3. Track trades specifically around your chosen events
4. Measure: Higher win rate? Better R-multiple? Acceptable volatility?

## Calendar Customization

### Filter by Country/Region
1. Click **"Filter"** on the calendar view
2. Select countries/regions of interest: US, Eurozone, UK, Japan, etc.
3. Calendar updates to show only events from selected regions
4. Useful if you trade only EUR pairs or focus on US markets

### Filter by Impact Level
1. Use filter options to show only High-Impact events
2. Or show Medium and Low-Impact for complete picture
3. Helps reduce calendar clutter if you only care about major announcements

### Subscribe to Notifications
1. Navigate to **Settings > Notifications**
2. Enable **"Economic Calendar Alerts"**
3. Select: High-Impact events, specific countries, or custom alerts
4. Receive notifications before/after major releases

## Important Events Calendar

### US Events (Major Impact)
- **Non-Farm Payroll (NFP):** First Friday of each month, 8:30 AM ET
- **Federal Reserve Interest Rate Decision:** 8 times per year, varies
- **Initial Jobless Claims:** Every Thursday, 8:30 AM ET
- **Core Inflation (CPI/PPI):** Monthly, around 8:30 AM ET

### Eurozone Events
- **ECB Interest Rate Decision:** 6 times per year, varies
- **Eurozone Inflation Data:** Monthly, around 10:00 AM CET
- **Eurozone GDP:** Quarterly releases

### UK Events
- **Bank of England Interest Rate Decision:** 8 times per year
- **UK Inflation Data:** Monthly
- **UK Retail Sales:** Monthly

### Other Major Economies
- **Japan:** BOJ Interest Rate, Nikkei manufacturing PMI
- **China:** Manufacturing PMI, GDP, Industrial Production
- **Canada:** Employment data, inflation, GDP

## Best Practices

**Schedule Review:** Check the economic calendar at the start of each trading week to plan your trading schedule.

**Mark Your Calendar:** If using external calendar, sync economic events so you don't accidentally trade through major announcements.

**Test Event Sensitivity:** Review your recent trades to see if you perform better on quiet days or event-driven days.

**Use Stop Losses:** Always have stops in place before major events in case of news gaps.

**Document Event Trades:** Tag trades taken around economic events so you can later analyze their performance separately from your regular trading.
