# Understanding Drawdown Widgets in TradeZella

Drawdown is a critical risk metric in trading. TradeZella provides three specialized widgets to help you track and understand different aspects of account decline.

## Cumulative Drawdown

### Location
The Cumulative Drawdown widget appears in the **lower section** of your dashboard.

### What It Measures
Cumulative Drawdown tracks the net P&L drawdown across your entire trading period, showing how much your account equity declined from its highest point across each trading day. This widget visualizes the progression of drawdown over time.

### Use Case
Use this metric to understand the pattern and progression of drawdown throughout your trading history and identify periods of significant account decline.

## Max Drawdown

### Location
The Max Drawdown widget appears in the **upper section** of your dashboard.

### What It Measures
Max Drawdown represents the single largest peak-to-trough decline in your account equity. It shows:
- The magnitude of the largest drawdown experienced
- The date when this maximum drawdown occurred

This is often considered the most important drawdown metric for evaluating risk.

### Use Case
Monitor Max Drawdown to understand your worst-case scenario and ensure your risk management strategies are adequate for actual market conditions.

## Average Drawdown

### Location
The Average Drawdown widget appears in the **upper section** of your dashboard.

### What It Measures
Average Drawdown calculates the mean drawdown magnitude across your entire trading timeframe. It provides a normalized measure of typical drawdown severity rather than focusing on the single worst instance.

### Use Case
Use Average Drawdown to assess the typical volatility and drawdown you experience relative to your typical wins and losses.

## View Variations

### Dollar vs Percentage Views

The calculations for drawdown metrics differ depending on your selected dashboard view:

- **Dollar View** - Drawdown expressed in absolute currency amounts (e.g., -$5,000)
- **Percentage View** - Drawdown expressed as a percentage of your account balance (e.g., -15%)

Both views measure the same drawdowns but normalize them differently. The percentage view is particularly useful when comparing accounts of different sizes.

## Understanding Drawdown in Context

All three drawdown widgets work together to give you a complete picture:
- **Max Drawdown** shows your worst experience
- **Average Drawdown** shows your typical experience
- **Cumulative Drawdown** shows how drawdown evolved over time
