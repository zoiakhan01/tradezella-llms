# Reports - Tags

The Tags Report in TradeZella allows you to analyze your trading performance based on custom tags you've assigned to trades. This powerful feature helps you evaluate the effectiveness of different trading approaches, instruments, and methodologies.

## Accessing the Tags Report

To view your tags report:

1. Log in to your TradeZella account
2. Navigate to "Reports" in the main menu
3. Click on "Tags" or "Tags Report"
4. Select your preferred date range
5. The report displays all tagged trades and performance metrics

## Understanding the Tags Report

The Tags Report shows:

- **Tag Name**: The custom tag label
- **Trade Count**: Total number of trades with that tag
- **Win Rate**: Percentage of profitable trades
- **P&L**: Total profit or loss for trades with that tag
- **Average Trade**: Mean profit/loss per trade
- **Largest Win**: Biggest profit on a tagged trade
- **Largest Loss**: Biggest loss on a tagged trade
- **Profit Factor**: Ratio of gross profit to gross loss

## Key Metrics Explained

### Trade Count

- Total number of trades assigned the tag
- Higher volume provides more statistical reliability
- Shows how frequently you use a particular tag
- Helps identify your most active trading approaches

### Win Rate

- Percentage of winning trades with that tag
- Helps identify consistency of an approach
- Shows which tags represent profitable setups
- Compares success rates across different tags

### P&L (Profit and Loss)

- Total net profit or loss for all trades with tag
- Shows cumulative performance
- Can be sorted ascending or descending
- Identifies your most and least profitable tags

### Average Trade

- Mean profit or loss per trade
- Shows consistency of approach
- Tags with higher averages are more reliable
- Helps identify quality over quantity

### Profit Factor

- Ratio of wins to losses (total profit ÷ total loss)
- Values above 1.5 indicate good profitability
- Values above 2.0 indicate excellent profitability
- Compares relative performance across tags

### Strategy Effectiveness

- Identify your most profitable approaches

## Sorting and Filtering

Customize your view:

### Sort Options

- Sort by tag name (alphabetical)
- Sort by trade count (highest to lowest)
- Sort by win rate
- Sort by total P&L
- Sort by average trade
- Sort by profit factor

### Filter Options

- Date range: Custom time periods
- Win/Loss filter: Show only profitable or losing tags
- Minimum trade count: Focus on statistically significant tags
- Profit range: Show tags within specific P&L ranges
- Specific tags: Display only selected tags

## Interpreting Results

Understanding what the data means:

### High Win Rate + High P&L
- Excellent tag representing strong performance
- Keep using this approach
- Consider increasing position size

### High Win Rate + Low P&L
- Good consistency, but small wins
- Trades may have favorable risk-reward
- Consider taking larger positions

### Low Win Rate + High P&L
- Few wins but they're very profitable
- High risk-reward ratio trades
- May indicate breakout or trend trading approach

### Low Win Rate + Low P&L
- Losing tag that should be avoided
- Review why trades consistently lose
- Consider removing or modifying this approach

## Using Tags for Accountability

Leverage tags for performance tracking:

- **Track Strategy Changes**: Tag improvements and see impact
- **Market Adaptation**: Tag by conditions and refine approach
- **Growth Tracking**: See improvement in win rates and P&L
- **Discipline Monitoring**: Tag trades following vs. breaking rules

## Tag Organization Best Practices

Organize tags strategically:

- **Single Criteria Per Tag**: One purpose per tag for clarity
- **Consistent Naming**: Use same naming convention
- **Limit Tag Count**: Too many tags dilutes analysis
- **Regular Review**: Retire unused or obsolete tags
- **Documentation**: Record what each tag means
- **Hierarchy**: Use tag prefixes (e.g., "session-london", "session-ny")

## Common Tag Categories

Consider these tagging approaches:

- **Strategy-Based**: "scalping", "swing-trade", "position-trade"
- **Market-Based**: "eurusd", "gbpusd", "sp500"
- **Condition-Based**: "trending", "ranging", "volatile"
- **Session-Based**: "london", "newyork", "tokyo"
- **Quality-Based**: "high-confidence", "low-conviction"
- **Setup-Based**: "breakout", "reversal", "support-bounce"
