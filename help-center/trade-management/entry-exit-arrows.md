# How Entry and Exit Arrows Are Displayed

Visual markers on your price charts help you quickly identify where you entered and exited trades. TradeZella uses directional arrows to clearly mark these critical points on your trading charts, making it easy to analyze your entry timing, exit decisions, and price action surrounding your trade execution. Understanding how these arrows are displayed helps you quickly review your trade setup and execution.

The entry and exit arrows provide instant visual feedback about your trade execution quality relative to the price movement that followed. By clearly marking these points, you can evaluate whether you entered at optimal prices, whether you held positions too long or exited too early, and identify patterns in your trading execution.

Arrow placement, color coding, and positioning all carry specific meaning that helps you understand your trades at a glance.

## Key Features/Steps

### Entry Arrow Display

Entry arrows are displayed on the price chart at the exact price level and time when you entered your position. For long positions (buys), the entry arrow typically appears as a green arrow pointing upward, indicating the direction of your intended trade. For short positions (sells), the entry arrow appears as a red or downward-pointing arrow, showing your intended direction was downward.

The arrow is positioned on the chart at the candle or time period corresponding to your entry execution time. If your order was filled across multiple candles or partial fills, the arrow marks the primary entry execution point. The arrow extends from the price level of your entry point, making it easy to see exactly what price you paid for your entry.

### Exit Arrow Display

Exit arrows mark your trade's conclusion, displayed at the price level and time of your exit execution. Exit arrows use a complementary color scheme: red or downward-pointing arrows typically indicate your exit from a long position (you sold at that level), and green or upward-pointing arrows indicate your exit from a short position (you covered at that level).

The exit arrow is clearly distinguished from the entry arrow, either through color differentiation or arrow style, allowing you to quickly identify both your entry and exit on the same chart. This visual separation helps you assess the complete trade arc from entry through exit.

### Long Position Markers

For long positions you've held (where you bought), the entry arrow visually indicates your buy point, typically displayed in green or as an upward-pointing arrow. Your exit arrow (the point where you sold) is displayed in a contrasting color, usually red or as a downward-pointing marker. This color distinction makes it immediately clear which was your entry and which was your exit for long trades.

### Short Position Markers

For short positions (where you sold short expecting downward movement), the entry arrow typically appears in red or as a downward-pointing arrow, indicating your short sale point. Your exit arrow (where you covered the short) is displayed as green or an upward-pointing arrow. Again, the color differentiation clearly shows the direction of your original trade intention versus your exit.

### Arrow Placement Logic

Arrows are positioned directly on the price bars or candlesticks where your execution occurred. If you entered on a specific candle, the entry arrow appears on or near that candle's price range. The arrow's vertical position corresponds to your actual entry price. Similarly, exit arrows appear on the candles matching your exit execution time and price level.

If your order wasn't filled immediately (for example, if you placed a limit order), the arrow appears at the candle where the trade actually filled, not where you originally submitted the order. This reflects your actual execution, not your intended entry point.

### Multiple Entries and Exits

Some traders scale into or out of positions across multiple executions. TradeZella may display multiple entry arrows if you had partial fills on entry, or multiple exit arrows if you exited your position in stages. Each arrow represents an actual execution, providing a complete visual record of how you built and unwound your position.

### Viewing Arrows on Different Timeframes

When you zoom in or out on your chart or change the timeframe, the entry and exit arrows remain visible and maintain their positioning. You can examine your execution at different scales, such as zooming in on a 5-minute chart to see exact execution details, or zooming out to a daily chart to see your trade in broader market context.
