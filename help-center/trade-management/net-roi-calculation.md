# Understanding NET ROI Calculation

Return on Investment (ROI) is a fundamental metric for evaluating trading performance. NET ROI specifically measures the percentage return on your trading capital after accounting for all costs and fees. Understanding how TradeZella calculates NET ROI helps you accurately assess your trading performance and compare results across different time periods and accounts.

TradeZella's NET ROI calculation follows industry-standard formulas and includes all factors that affect your actual returns. By understanding the components of this calculation, you can better evaluate your trading strategy's effectiveness and identify areas for improvement in your risk management and trade execution.

The NET ROI metric provides a clear picture of how efficiently you're using your trading capital, making it one of the most important performance indicators tracked in your trading journal.

## Key Features/Steps

### NET ROI Formula

TradeZella calculates NET ROI using the following formula:

NET ROI = (Net P&L / Account Balance) × 100

Where "Net P&L" represents your total profit or loss after accounting for all deductions, and "Account Balance" is your starting capital or current account balance depending on the calculation period. This formula expresses your returns as a percentage of capital deployed, making it easy to compare performance across different account sizes.

### Components of the Calculation

Net P&L includes all profits and losses from your trades minus any commissions, fees, and other transaction costs. Your Account Balance represents the initial capital you're trading with. When calculating ROI for a specific period, TradeZella uses the account balance at the beginning of that period. This ensures that percentage returns are accurately reflected relative to the capital you had available.

### Commissions and Fees

Commissions paid to brokers, platform fees, and other trading-related costs are automatically deducted from your gross profit to calculate your net profit. If your broker provides commissions data, TradeZella automatically includes these in the calculation. For cases where commissions aren't automatically imported, you can manually add them to individual trades using the "Add Commission" feature on the trade details page.

### Multiple Account Considerations

When you have multiple trading accounts, NET ROI is calculated individually for each account. Each account's ROI reflects only the trades and capital in that specific account. Your overall ROI across all accounts is calculated as a weighted average based on the capital in each account. This allows you to compare performance between different accounts and trading strategies.

### Time Period Calculations

NET ROI can be calculated for various time periods: daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, or for your entire trading history. When viewing ROI for a specific period, TradeZella uses the account balance at the start of that period as the denominator. This ensures consistent and accurate comparison of returns across different timeframes.

### Interpreting Your NET ROI

A positive NET ROI indicates profitable trading, while a negative ROI indicates losses. For example, a NET ROI of 5% means you've earned 5% on your initial capital after all fees. Remember that ROI alone doesn't account for risk or the time it took to achieve returns, so consider it alongside other metrics like win rate and risk-to-reward ratios.
