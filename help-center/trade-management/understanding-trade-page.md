# Understanding the Trade Page

The Trade Page is your comprehensive view of a single trade, displaying all relevant information and tools for analyzing and managing that specific trade. When you click on any trade from your Trade Log, the Trade Page opens to show detailed data including price action via an interactive chart, entry and exit points, comprehensive statistics, and options for adding notes or screenshots.

Understanding all the elements on the Trade Page helps you effectively review your trades, document your decision-making process, and identify patterns in your trading behavior. The Trade Page serves as both a detailed record of each trade and a powerful analysis tool for continuous improvement.

Each Trade Page includes interactive features that allow you to annotate the chart, add execution details, manage profit targets and stop losses, and collaborate with other traders through shared links.

## Key Features/Steps

### Trade Chart Display

The centerpiece of the Trade Page is an interactive price chart showing the instrument's price action during and around your trade period. The chart displays candlesticks or bars with clear visual markers indicating your entry and exit points. You can zoom, scroll, and adjust the timeframe of the chart to see more context around your trade execution. The chart includes entry arrows (typically green for long positions) and exit arrows (typically red) marking your exact execution points.

### Entry and Exit Information

Below or alongside the chart, you'll find detailed information about your entry: the date and time, the price level, and the order type. Similarly, your exit details show the exit date, time, and price, along with any notes about the exit decision. If you used limit orders, stop orders, or other special order types, this information is displayed here. The interface clearly shows whether your trade was a long or short position.

### Trade Statistics and Metrics

The Trade Page displays comprehensive statistics for the trade including: gross profit/loss, net profit/loss (after commissions), percentage gain or loss, the number of days the trade was held, bars held (if applicable), and risk-to-reward ratios if you've set profit targets and stop losses. These metrics give you a quick overview of the trade's performance and help you evaluate the trade's quality relative to your setup.

### Profit Targets and Stop Losses

A dedicated section allows you to set or view profit target levels and stop loss levels for your trade. These are essential for calculating R-multiples and evaluating risk management in your trades. If you enter your intended profit target and stop loss prices, TradeZella calculates the potential risk and reward, giving you the R-multiple for the trade setup.

### Tags and Categorization

You can assign tags to trades to categorize them by strategy, market condition, day of week, or any other classification system. Tags help you filter and analyze trades later, allowing you to see performance breakdowns by different categories. Use tags consistently to build meaningful patterns across your trading history.

### Notes and Trade Rationale

The notes section allows you to document why you took the trade, what setups you identified, what went right or wrong, and lessons learned. These notes become invaluable when reviewing your trading journal and identifying patterns. You can add or edit notes at any time, providing a space for detailed commentary beyond the quantitative metrics.

### Screenshots Section

Attach screenshots or images to document the chart setup before you entered the trade, the trade in progress, or the final exit. Screenshots provide visual evidence of the setup you identified and help reinforce your decision-making process when reviewing the trade later.

### Executions Table

A detailed table shows every order execution for the trade, including fills at different price levels if your order was filled in multiple batches. This is particularly useful for understanding how your actual fills compared to your intended entry and exit prices.
