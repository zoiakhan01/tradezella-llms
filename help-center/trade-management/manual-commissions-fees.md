# How to Manually Add Commissions & Fees

Accurate profit and loss tracking requires accounting for all trading costs, including commissions, platform fees, and other expenses. While TradeZella can automatically import commission data from some brokers, there are situations where you need to manually add or adjust commission amounts on your trades. This ensures your NET ROI and other performance metrics reflect your true profitability.

Commissions and fees directly impact your actual returns. Even small commission amounts can add up across many trades and significantly affect your overall performance calculations. TradeZella's manual commission feature gives you precise control over these costs, ensuring your records accurately reflect what your broker charged you.

Whether your broker doesn't automatically provide commission data, you made trades on a platform that TradeZella doesn't integrate with, or you need to correct incorrect automatic imports, the manual commission feature provides complete flexibility.

## Key Features/Steps

### Access Trade Details

Navigate to your Trade Log and find the trade for which you need to add or modify commission information. Click on the trade to open its Trade Page. On the Trade Page, look for a section labeled "Commissions," "Fees," or "Costs." This section typically appears on the right side of the page or in a details panel below the chart.

If you don't immediately see a commission field, check if there's an "Edit" button or pencil icon next to the trade details that allows you to enter additional information. Some versions of TradeZella show this information in an expandable "Advanced" or "More Details" section.

### Add Commission Information

Click on the commission field or the "Add Commission" button to enable editing. You'll see an input field where you can enter the commission amount in dollars or your local currency. Enter the exact commission amount that your broker charged you for the trade. If you had multiple commissions (for example, one on entry and one on exit), you can either add them together or enter them separately if TradeZella supports separate entry and exit commission fields.

Some brokers charge a commission amount; others charge it as a percentage of the trade value. If your broker charges a percentage-based commission, calculate the dollar amount before entering it into TradeZella. For example, if your trade was $10,000 and your broker charged 0.1%, the commission would be $10.

### Edit Existing Commissions

If TradeZella has already imported a commission amount automatically or you previously entered incorrect commission data, you can modify it. Click on the existing commission amount to edit it, clear the field, and enter the correct amount. This is useful if you realize an automatic import was incorrect or if your broker corrected charges after initial reporting.

### Categorize Different Fee Types

Some advanced commission tracking allows you to categorize different types of fees separately: brokerage commissions, exchange fees, clearing fees, or other charges. If your TradeZella version supports this, you can enter each type separately for detailed record-keeping. This level of detail helps you understand exactly where your trading costs come from.

### Update Total P&L

After adding or modifying commission amounts, TradeZella automatically recalculates your NET P&L for that trade. The trade's profit or loss figure will be adjusted downward by the commission amount you entered. For example, if your trade had a $100 gross profit but you paid $5 in commissions, your NET P&L will display as $95.

### View Impact on Overall Statistics

Once you've entered commission data, your account-level statistics will update accordingly. Your NET ROI, total NET P&L, and other performance metrics will reflect the commissions you've documented. This provides an accurate picture of your true trading profitability after all costs.

### Bulk Commission Updates

If you have multiple trades that need commission updates, some versions of TradeZella may support bulk editing. Check if you can select multiple trades and apply commission adjustments to all of them at once, which is more efficient than updating individual trades.
