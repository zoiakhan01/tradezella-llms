# How to Set Profit Targets and Stop Losses

Profit targets and stop losses are fundamental risk management tools that define your trade's potential risk and reward. Setting these levels on your trades in TradeZella allows you to calculate important metrics like risk-to-reward ratio and R-multiples, which help you evaluate your trade quality and edge as a trader. Understanding how to effectively set and use these levels improves your trading analysis and decision-making process.

Even if you didn't pre-define these levels when you entered your trade, documenting them on the Trade Page provides valuable context for reviewing your trade decisions. These levels help you understand what you were risking and what you expected to gain, allowing you to evaluate whether your actual results matched your planned risk parameters.

Setting profit targets and stop losses transforms your trade documentation from simple entry and exit recording into a comprehensive risk analysis tool.

## Key Features/Steps

### Access the Profit Target and Stop Loss Section

Open any trade from your Trade Log to view its Trade Page. Look for a section dedicated to profit targets and stop losses, typically displayed on the right side panel or in a details section. This section might be labeled "Risk/Reward," "Target/Stop Loss," or "Risk Parameters." If you don't immediately see this section, check for an expandable "More Details" or "Advanced" section.

Click on this section to edit the values. You'll see input fields where you can enter your profit target price and your stop loss price. Some versions display these alongside your entry price for easy reference and calculation.

### Enter Your Stop Loss Price

In the stop loss field, enter the price level at which you would have stopped out of the trade if your predetermined risk management rules were applied. The stop loss price represents the maximum loss you were willing to accept on the trade. For long positions, this is typically below your entry price. For short positions, this is typically above your entry price.

Be honest about what your actual stop loss would have been, even if you didn't have a physical stop order in place with your broker. Document the price level that represents your maximum acceptable loss for this trade. This level is critical for calculating your actual risk exposure and R-multiples.

### Enter Your Profit Target Price

In the profit target field, enter the price level at which you would take profits or at which you identified a logical take-profit point. The profit target price represents where you expected to exit your winning trade. For long positions, this is typically above your entry price. For short positions, this is typically below your entry price.

If you had multiple profit targets at different price levels, some versions of TradeZella may allow you to enter multiple targets. Alternatively, you can enter your primary profit target or an average of your intended exit targets.

### Automatic R-Multiple Calculation

Once you've entered your entry price, stop loss price, and profit target price, TradeZella automatically calculates the R-multiple for your trade. The R-multiple represents how many times your risk you were potentially rewarded.

The calculation works as follows: R = (Profit Target - Entry Price) / (Entry Price - Stop Loss). For example, if you entered at $100, had a stop loss at $95 (risking $5), and a profit target at $110 (potential $10 profit), your R-multiple would be 2R (you risked 1 to make 2).

### View Risk-to-Reward Ratio

Beyond the R-multiple, TradeZella displays your risk-to-reward ratio, showing you at a glance whether the potential reward justified the risk. A favorable risk-to-reward ratio is typically 1:2 or better, meaning you risk $1 to potentially make $2 or more. This metric helps you evaluate whether individual trades met your quality standards.

### Compare Actual vs. Planned Results

With your profit targets and stop losses documented, you can compare your planned risk-reward scenario with your actual results. If your actual exit was significantly different from your profit target, analyze why. Did you exit too early or too late? Did you let winners run further than planned, or did you miss your target by exiting prematurely? These comparisons reveal insights about your trade execution and decision-making.

### Filtering Trades by Risk-to-Reward

Once you've populated profit targets and stop losses across your trades, you can filter and analyze your trades based on their R-multiples. Review how profitable you are specifically on trades with good risk-to-reward setups versus unfavorable ones. This analysis reveals whether your edge comes from high-probability trades, high-reward setups, or a combination.

### Edit or Update Risk Parameters

You can edit your profit target and stop loss prices at any time. If you realize you documented incorrect levels, simply click on the field and update it. TradeZella will automatically recalculate your R-multiple and other related metrics based on the new values.
