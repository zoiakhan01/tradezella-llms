# How to Export Data to CSV from Trade Log

Exporting your trade data to CSV format provides flexibility for external analysis, backup purposes, or integration with other trading tools and spreadsheet applications. TradeZella makes it easy to export your complete trading history or selected trades with all relevant metrics included in the CSV file.

CSV (Comma-Separated Values) is a widely supported format that opens in Excel, Google Sheets, and most other data analysis tools. By exporting your trades to CSV, you can perform custom analysis, create additional reports, maintain local backups, or transfer data to other platforms. The export function preserves all key information about your trades while providing a portable format.

TradeZella's export feature includes comprehensive trade data such as profit and loss calculations, entry and exit prices, execution dates, account information, symbols traded, and any custom tags or notes you've added to your trades.

## Key Features/Steps

### Access the Export Function

Open the Trade Log page from your TradeZella dashboard. Look for the export button, typically located in the toolbar at the top of the Trade Log table. The export button is usually represented by a download icon or labeled "Export." Some versions may include the export option in a menu button (three dots) alongside other Trade Log actions.

### Select Trades to Export

You have two options for exporting: export all trades or export selected trades. To export all trades visible in the current Trade Log, simply click the export button without selecting specific trades. To export only certain trades, use the checkboxes next to individual trade entries to select them first. You can select as many trades as needed, and TradeZella will include only those selected trades in the export file.

### Configure Export Settings

Depending on your TradeZella version, you may see options to customize what data is included in the export. Common settings include choosing which columns to include, date range filtering, and account selection. Review these options to ensure the exported data meets your needs. For most users, the default settings include all essential trade information.

### Download the CSV File

Click the "Export" or "Download CSV" button to initiate the file download. Your browser will download the CSV file with a filename typically in the format "TradeZella_Trades_[Date].csv" or similar. The file will be saved to your default downloads folder on your computer.

### Using the Exported Data

Open the CSV file in Excel, Google Sheets, or your preferred spreadsheet application. The file will contain columns for each trade's key metrics: entry date, exit date, entry price, exit price, profit/loss amount, profit/loss percentage, symbol, account name, tags, notes, and more. You can now sort, filter, and analyze your trades using familiar spreadsheet functions and formulas.
