# Understanding MAE and MFE — Maximizing Trade Potential

MAE and MFE are critical metrics that help you understand whether you're leaving money on the table or using stop losses that are too tight.

## Maximum Adverse Excursion (MAE)

MAE represents the lowest price point reached during your trade (highest price point for short trades). This metric shows the most significant unrealized loss your trade experienced at any point. Understanding your MAE helps you evaluate whether your stop loss placements are appropriately positioned.

## Maximum Favorable Excursion (MFE)

MFE represents the highest price point reached during your trade (lowest price point for short trades). This metric shows the most significant unrealized profit available at any point during the trade. Comparing your actual profit to your MFE reveals whether you're exiting trades too early or holding them long enough to capture optimal gains.

## Using MAE and MFE

These metrics are available on trade detail pages and in your reports. By analyzing the gap between MAE, MFE, and your actual exit price, you can refine your entry timing, stop loss placement, and profit-taking strategy.
