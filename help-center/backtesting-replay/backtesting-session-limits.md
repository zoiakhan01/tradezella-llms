# Is There a Limit on the Quantity of Backtesting Sessions I Can Create?

Yes, TradeZella implements session limits based on your subscription plan to ensure platform stability and optimize resource allocation. Here's what you need to know about backtesting session limits.

## Plan-Based Session Limits

### Free Plan
- **Active sessions**: 1 concurrent backtesting session
- **Total sessions**: 5 total backtesting sessions
- **Storage**: 30 days data retention
- **Best for**: Trying backtesting features, learning basics

The Free plan allows you to create up to 5 sessions total, but only 1 can be active at a time. After 30 days, sessions are automatically archived.

### Essential Plan
- **Active sessions**: 3 concurrent backtesting sessions
- **Total sessions**: 50 total backtesting sessions
- **Storage**: 6 months data retention
- **Historical data**: Up to 5 years lookback

The Essential plan is ideal for serious traders developing strategies. You can maintain 50 sessions with higher storage duration, and multiple sessions can run concurrently.

### Pro Plan
- **Active sessions**: Unlimited concurrent backtesting sessions
- **Total sessions**: Unlimited backtesting sessions
- **Storage**: Unlimited data retention
- **Historical data**: 20+ years lookback

The Pro plan offers complete freedom to create and manage as many sessions as needed without any restrictions.

## What Counts as a "Session"?

A backtesting session is counted when:
- You create a new session with specific parameters
- The session contains active or completed trades
- The session is saved in your account

Sessions that are counted:
- Completed sessions (with trades executed)
- In-progress sessions (currently trading)
- Paused sessions (temporarily inactive)
- Archived sessions (stored for later)

Sessions that DON'T count:
- Deleted sessions (permanently removed)
- Session templates or copies in creation

## Managing Your Session Quota

### Checking Your Current Usage

1. Go to **Backtesting** in the main menu
2. Look at the **Session Summary** section
3. View "X of Y sessions used" counter
4. See storage usage for your plan

### Staying Within Limits

**For Free and Essential Plans:**
1. Delete old, completed sessions you no longer need
2. Archive important sessions for reference
3. Consolidate multiple short sessions into comprehensive ones
4. Monitor your usage regularly

**Organization Tips:**
- Name sessions descriptively for easy identification
- Use session tags to organize by strategy
- Archive completed analysis sessions
- Keep only active development sessions active

## Upgrading Your Plan

### When You Need More Sessions

If you're approaching your session limit:
1. **Upgrade to Essential**: Increases to 50 total sessions
2. **Upgrade to Pro**: Unlimited sessions and storage
3. **Review usage**: Delete unnecessary sessions before upgrading
4. **Plan strategically**: Consider your backtesting workflow

### Upgrade Process

1. Go to **Settings > Billing & Plan**
2. Click **Upgrade** on your desired plan
3. Complete payment information
4. Upgrade applies immediately
5. Access new limits instantly

## Session Storage and Retention

### Data Retention Policies

| Plan | Retention Period | Data Storage |
|------|-----------------|--------------|
| Free | 30 days | Limited |
| Essential | 6 months | 100 GB per plan |
| Pro | Unlimited | Unlimited |

### Auto-Archive Features

- Free: Sessions auto-archived after 30 days inactive
- Essential: Sessions kept for 6 months
- Pro: Permanent storage (until manually deleted)

## Best Practices for Session Management

### Organization Strategy
1. **Current projects**: Keep active development sessions
2. **Reference library**: Archive completed strategy sessions
3. **Testing archive**: Save completed test sessions for learning
4. **Clean periodically**: Delete failed experiments after review

### Naming Conventions
- Use descriptive names: "ES_Breakout_Feb2024_v2"
- Include strategy name
- Add date or version number
- Makes filtering and organization easier

### Regular Maintenance
- Review sessions monthly
- Delete unused or test sessions
- Archive successful strategies
- Keep workspace clean and organized

## Exceeding Your Limit

### What Happens When You Hit Your Limit

- You cannot create new sessions
- Error message appears when attempting to create
- Existing sessions remain accessible
- You can still view and analyze completed sessions

### Solution

You have three options:
1. **Delete sessions**: Remove old or unnecessary sessions
2. **Archive sessions**: Move completed sessions to archive
3. **Upgrade plan**: Get higher limits with a plan upgrade

## Special Circumstances

### Academic and Professional Licensing

Organizations needing higher limits for training:
- Contact TradeZella support for enterprise plans
- Volume discounts available
- Custom session limits possible
- Dedicated support included

### Session Consolidation

If approaching limits, consider:
- Combining test sessions into one
- Using markers to delineate multiple tests
- Creating session variants as trades within one session
