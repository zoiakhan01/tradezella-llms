# What are Groups and Rules in Strategies

## Overview

Groups and Rules form the foundation of your trading strategy definition, allowing you to organize complex trading logic and track adherence to your predetermined framework.

## Understanding Groups

## Organization Structure

Groups act as containers that organize related rules into logical categories:

- Entry Criteria - Rules governing position entry conditions
- Exit Criteria - Rules determining position closure
- Market Conditions - Environmental constraints affecting trade validity

## Managing Rules

## Rule Definition

Each rule describes a specific condition or pattern within your trading framework.

## Criteria Options

When defining rules, specify when they apply:

- Show for winners - Rule applies to profitable trades
- Show for losers - Rule applies to losing trades
- Show for breakeven - Rule applies to flat trades
- Always - Rule applies to all trades regardless of outcome

## Editing and Adding Rules

## Modifying Existing Rules

Access the Rules tab within a strategy, click the three-dot menu, and select Edit to modify existing rule definitions.

## Creating New Rules

Click the "+" button to add new rules to your strategy, then define conditions and criteria for each new rule.

## Rule Adherence Tracking

## Performance Metrics

When checking off rules on individual trades, TradeZella tracks:

- Follow rate % - Percentage of trades following each rule
- Net P&L - Profit/loss for trades following specific rules
- Profit factor - Win rate for rule-following trades
- Win rate - Success rate when rules are followed
