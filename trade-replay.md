# TradeZella Trade Replay

Replay your actual trades tick-by-tick. Slow down critical moments, spot execution errors, and see what actually happened — with entries, exits, and context visible on the chart.

## Replay Modes

Three ways to review your trades, each designed for a different level of analysis.

- **Trade Replay** — Select any trade from your journal and watch it unfold from pre-entry to final exit. See every tick that led to your decision
- **Day Replay** — Replay your entire trading session. Understand how your first loss affected your second entry. Compare early session vs. late session performance
- **Scenario Replay** (Coming Soon) — Review scenario-based performance, compare similar setups, and build pattern recognition

## Playback & Navigation

Control time and jump to any moment in your trade.

- **Second-by-Second Replay** — Relive any trade exactly as it happened, candle by candle, at any interval
- **Adjustable Replay Speed** — Speed up through noise or slow down through critical price action
- **Multiple Timeframes** — See how higher timeframe context influenced your lower timeframe entries
- **Jump to Any Execution** — Click any entry, exit, or scale to jump directly to that exact moment
- **Entry & Exit Markers** — Green and red markers clearly show your executions on the chart
- **Select Specific Trades** — Choose exactly which trades to include in your replay session

## Integrated Journaling

Tag, annotate, and journal without leaving the replay.

- **Tag Mistakes in Real-Time** — Create custom tags the moment something clicks. Build a library of mistakes, conditions, or patterns
- **Check Against Your Plan** — Review whether each trade fit your rules and whether your entry was valid
- **Add Notes While Replaying** — Write observations or journal entries directly while watching your trade unfold. Attach screenshots for context
- **Journal a Trade or Full Day** — Log notes on individual trades or reflect on the full session

## Trade Replay vs. Backtesting

Both are valuable but serve different purposes.

**Backtesting** is for strategy validation — testing ideas on historical data you may or may not have traded. It helps you prove if a strategy has a statistical edge before risking real capital.

**Trade Replay** is for execution review — replaying trades you actually took with real capital and real emotions. It helps you see what you did wrong, what you did right, and what you missed.

Use backtesting to develop your strategy. Use Trade Replay to refine your execution. Learn more at [TradeZella Backtesting](https://www.tradezella.com/backtesting).

## Why Review Your Trades?

- **All in One Place** — No switching between TradingView, spreadsheets, and your journal. Everything in one workflow
- **Review Your Day in Minutes** — Replay, tag, and journal your full session in 15–20 minutes
- **Data That Compounds** — Every tag and note feeds into your reports. The more you review, the smarter your analytics get
- **Build a Habit That Sticks** — Consistent review turns losses into lessons and lessons into long-term improvement

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is Trade Replay?**
Trade Replay lets you replay your actual trades tick-by-tick inside TradeZella. You can review individual trades or your entire trading day with full chart context, entry/exit markers, and integrated journaling.

**How is Trade Replay different from backtesting?**
Backtesting tests strategies on historical data. Trade Replay reviews trades you actually took — with your real entries, exits, emotions, and decisions. Both are available in TradeZella.

**Can I journal while replaying?**
Yes. Add notes, tags, screenshots, and ratings directly while watching your trade unfold. Everything syncs to your journal and reports automatically.

**Does TradeZella trade for me?**
No. TradeZella is not a brokerage. You execute trades on your broker and TradeZella imports them for tracking, analysis, and replay.

**Is Trade Replay available on all plans?**
Trade Replay is available on the Pro plan. Essential plan does not include Trade Replay.
